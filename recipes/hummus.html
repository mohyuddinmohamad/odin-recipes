Hummus with Beef

Description

Hummus is a dip of Arab origin. It is a staple in Middle Eastern cuisine and can be paired with a variety of other foods. Hummus is very customizable in that there are a variety of different garnishes you can use. It is most commonly enjoyed with a garnish of olive oil, whole chickpeas, parsley, and paprika.

Recipe credit: The Golden Balance (YouTube)

Ingredients

  • 400 Grams of Dry Chickpeas
  • 150 Grams of Sesame Seed Paste
  • 1 Lemon
  • Salt to Taste
  • 1/4 to 1/2 Cup of Cold Water
  • 1/4 Cup Ghee
  • 1/2 lb Lean Beef

Optional

  • Garlic
  • Olie Oil
  • Cumin
  • Paprika

Directions

  1. Add your dry chickpeas to a bowl and submerge them in water. Let them soak and hydrate over night.
  2. Remove the water and transfer the chickpeas to a large pot. By now the chickpeas should have doubled in size.
  3. Submerge the chickpeas in the pot with clean water. Turn the stove up to a high heat and bring the water a light bubble.
  4. Add a pinch of baking soda to accelerate the cooking process. Do not add to much or it will give your hummus a bitter taste.
  5. As the chickpeas begin to cook, foam will start rising to the top. Remove the foam.
  6. When the water starts to vigorously bubble, drop the temperature to low and let the chickpeas cook for 2 hours.
  7. Once the chickpeas are cooked they should be nice and tender. You can confirm by taking one chickpeas and if it easly mashed between your fingers they are ready.
  8. Transfer your cooked chickpeas to a new container and store them in the freezer until frozen.
  9. Add your frozen chickpeas to a food processor.
  10. Add the sesame paste to the food processor.
  11. Before add the rest of the ingredients, give the food processor a quick pulse, and then scrape the remainnig off the sides of the food processor.At this point it will still be dry and clumpy.
  12. To the food processor, add the juice of 1 lemon, 1 teaspoon of cold water, and a generous pinch of salt. Blend the contents till the hummus is smooth.
  13. Plate your hummus and create a crater in the middle of the hummus. This is where you will add your beef.
  14. In a scorching hot pan add the ghee.
  15. Once the ghee is sizzling, add the beef (cubed pieces are easier to eat).
  16. Add a hefty pinch of salt.
  17. Once the beef is cooked and has a nice glaze, add the contents of the pan (the beef and ghee) to the crater made in the hummus.
